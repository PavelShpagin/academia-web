Academia StabX is optical flight stabilization for UAVs, operating day and night without GPS, compass, or external signals.

Here's how it works.

A downward-facing camera tracks terrain features in real time, computing optical flow — the apparent motion of ground patterns between frames — and fusing this with IMU data to derive velocity and position. Heading is maintained through visual odometry alone. The result is a drone that holds position, maintains orientation, and navigates home entirely through what it sees.

StabX activates at takeoff and runs parallel to manual control — pilots fly normally, the system intervenes only when needed. On signal loss, RTL engages automatically via the shortest trajectory to the operator.

Security is built in: activation requires a Bluetooth key, software is encrypted and bound to specific hardware. If a drone is lost, the system doesn't work for anyone else.

Why this matters.

StabX automates the hardest parts of UAV operation — reducing operator workload, increasing mission success rate, and making complex flight accessible to any pilot.

The system is deployed with hundreds of Armed Forces of Ukraine units across dozens of platforms — reconnaissance, relay, strike — operating daily in the most demanding operational environment on the planet. What works here will work anywhere.

We're preparing to expand from Ukraine to Europe and the United States. Our roadmap is clear: StabX will support any platform, any mission, any operator.
